The Girard-Perregaux Neo Bridges Aston Martin Edition Is Driven By Passion To Challenge Convention

article by SponsoredPost

When Girard-Perregaux announced its partnership in 2021 with legendary British luxury sports car maker Aston Martin, it seemed like an obvious and natural pairing. Two iconic marques with rich heritages, both of which consistently deliver at the apex of design and engineering. Only two years on and the venture has already produced four timepieces spanning several GP collections, with all but the original brilliantly incorporating the automaker’s recognizable Aston Martin Green. While the past three releases have built off the historic Laureato models, the newest offering from the two brands returns to the roots of the first model they produced, the Girard-Perregaux Bridges collection. The new Girard-Perregaux Neo Bridges Aston Martin Edition delivers a breathtaking modern watch, inspired by the sculptural Aston Martin DB12, and steeped in history, craftsmanship, and the combined engineering expertise of two luxury brands.

While the Laureato, Laureato Chronograph, and Laureato Absolute Chronograph models made in partnership with Aston Martin have been beautiful, modern timepieces, the new Girard-Perregaux Neo Bridges Aston Martin Edition manages to strike a balance between Girard-Perregaux’s past and its forward-looking present, while still effortlessly blending the spirit of Aston Martin’s DB12. As the name suggests, the watch puts a contemporary spin on Constant Girard’s 1897 design that utilized three parallel bridges and infused the architectural dial with striking accents of Aston Martin Green, while the sleek silhouette of the cases evokes the aerodynamic curves of the DB12.

Arsène Lippens Debuts With The Visionario Watch Collection

article by SponsoredPost

For all its storytelling, watchmaking can often be an impersonal affair. Among the most glorified timepieces, many are wrapped in tales of exploits performed by those we’ll never meet: space travelers, adventurers, racing legends, and heroes of wartime derring-do. Of the watches outside this intrepid realm, many align themselves with poetic contexts, sometimes hyper-specific (sunrise over the slopes of Mt. Iwate, anyone?). The remaining watches typically draw upon updated archival designs that, while often beautiful, lack a certain je ne sais quoi with regard to who was actually involved. Sure, a neo-vintage piece is nice. But what else makes it special aside from the glassy-eyed nostalgic “remember when” sentiments that it triggers?

The Visionario collection, aside from its familial origins, is special for its technical and design cohesiveness. Using an uncommon automatic chronograph caliber produced a throw from the Arsène Lippens’ headquarters in Neuchâtel, the watches set themselves apart at an early stage. Design-wise, they echo the sort of styling found on other neo-vintage pieces with recognizable qualities like Breguet numerals, paired with unique ones such as the automotive-inspired bezel. In another personalized touch, the double-step look comes from the similarly decorative appearance of the front grill on 1941 Pontiac Streamliners. Fortunately, the Visionario’s specs are a bit more svelte than the hulking 2.5-ton coupe with a 115” wheelbase.

New Release: Urwerk UR-230 Eagle Watch

article by Celine Simon

After saying goodbye to the UR-210 in 2020 and the UR-220 in 2022, Urwerk now welcomes a new model to the popular UR-200 series. Meet the Urwerk UR-230 “Eagle” watch, which retains many of the signature details of its progenitors but also boasts a slew of updates that will no doubt please fans of the independent high-horology watch brand.

Right off the bat, we see the familiarity of the trapeze-shaped case; yet, it’s different thanks to its new protective cover that can be lifted 90 degrees or locked into place when needed. As is customary for Urwerk watches, the UR-230 is sizable, measuring 44.81mm across, 55mm in length, and 18.38mm thick. Both the case and cover are fashioned from Carbon Thin Ply (CTP) carbon and black DLC-treated titanium. The black CTP carbon is machined in superimposed layers (318 layers in total) and pressure-bonded at high temperatures, which yields that distinctive pattern. To ensure easy lifting of the cover, the beak-shaped edge includes a finger grip; plus, there’s also a brake system to slow it down when closing to avoid any damage to the sapphire crystal it serves to protect. The case is water resistant to 30 meters.
